 # Apple iTunes Music Store Analysis
SQL Data Analysis Project

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the Apple iTunes music store dataset using SQL to uncover insights about customer behavior, music preferences, and sales performance.

The analysis focuses on understanding customer purchasing patterns, identifying popular tracks and artists, evaluating sales performance, and discovering geographic trends.

## Problem Statement

Apple iTunes manages a large digital music store containing thousands of tracks and customers across different regions.
As the business grows, understanding customer purchasing behavior, product popularity, and sales trends becomes essential.
This project uses SQL to analyze the iTunes relational database and generate insights that support data-driven business decisions.

## Dataset Description

The dataset consists of multiple relational tables including:

Customer – customer information  
Employee – support representatives  
Invoice – purchase transactions  
Invoice_Line – track purchases  
Track – songs in the store  
Album – album details  
Artist – music artists  
Genre – music genres  
Playlist – playlists of songs  

These tables are connected using primary and foreign keys to represent relationships between customers, purchases, and music content.

## Database Setup
The database for this project was created using MySQL.

The following steps were performed to set up the database:

1. Created relational tables using SQL scripts.
2. Imported CSV files containing the dataset into MySQL.
3. Established primary and foreign key relationships between tables.
4. Verified data integrity after loading the data.

This relational structure allows efficient querying and analysis of the music store data.

## Customer & Revenue Analysis
This section focuses on understanding customer spending patterns and overall revenue performance.

Key analyses performed include:

- Identifying top spending customers
- Calculating average customer lifetime value
- Determining repeat vs one-time customers
- Measuring total store revenue
- Analyzing revenue by country
  
The analysis reveals that a small number of customers contribute significantly to overall revenue, highlighting the importance of retaining high-value customers.

## Product, Artist & Genre Analysis
This analysis explores the popularity of music content available in the store.

Key insights include:

- Identifying top-selling tracks
- Determining the most popular artists
- Analyzing genre performance by sales and revenue
- Finding tracks that have never been purchased
- Measuring track distribution across genres
  
The results show that certain genres such as rock dominate both sales volume and revenue.
Artists like Queen and Jimi Hendrix generate some of the highest revenue, indicating strong listener demand for classic rock content.

## Sales, Employee & Geographic Analysis
This section evaluates operational performance and geographic sales trends.

Key analysis include:

- Revenue trends across different months
- Revenue generated by support representatives
- Number of customers managed by each employee
- Customer distribution across countries
- Revenue contribution by region
  
The results indicate that a small number of support representatives manage most customers and contribute significantly to total revenue.

Additionally, certain geographic regions generate higher customer engagement and sales.

## Customer Behavior & Purchase Patterns

This final analysis focuses on deeper customer purchasing behavior.

Key analyses performed include:

- Purchase frequency per customer
- Average invoice value per customer
- Total spending per customer
- Customers purchasing multiple genres
- First and most recent purchase dates
- Most purchased tracks and albums
- Tracks frequently bought together
The findings reveal that many customers purchase music from multiple genres, indicating diverse listening preferences.

Additionally, certain tracks and albums are frequently purchased together, suggesting opportunities for improved recommendation systems.

## Key Business Insights

From the analysis, several important insights were identified:

1. A small number of high-value customers generate a large portion of revenue.
2. Rock music dominates sales across multiple metrics.
3. Certain artists consistently generate high revenue.
4. Customer purchases often include multiple genres.
5. Some tracks and albums are frequently bought together.

These insights help understand both customer behavior and product performance within the iTunes store.

## Business Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following strategies are recommended:

- Focus marketing campaigns on high-performing genres such as rock.
- Develop personalized recommendation systems based on customer listening preferences.
- Introduce loyalty programs for high-value customers.
- Promote popular artists and tracks through curated playlists.
- Target high-performing geographic regions with localized marketing strategies.

Implementing these strategies could improve customer engagement and increase overall revenue.

## Conclusion
This project demonstrates how SQL-based data analysis can uncover valuable insights from a digital music store dataset.

By analyzing customer behavior, product popularity, and sales performance, the study provides meaningful insights that can support better business decision-making.

These findings can help improve marketing strategies, optimize product offerings, and enhance the overall customer experience within the iTunes music store.
